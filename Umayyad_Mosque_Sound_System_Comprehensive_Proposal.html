During the site assessment, frequency response measurements were taken at multiple listening positions throughout the prayer hall using the existing fragmented speaker system. These measurements reveal the severe acoustic inconsistencies caused by mixing incompatible equipment from 9 different manufacturers.

Key Findings from Acoustic Measurements:

  • Severe High-Frequency Rolloff: Existing Dynacord and Inter-M speakers show 10-15 dB loss above 8 kHz, destroying speech intelligibility
  • Inconsistent Left/Right Response: Significant asymmetry between paired speakers indicates component failures
  • Critical Band Problems: The 2-5 kHz band (essential for speech clarity) shows 5-8 dB variance across positions
  • Reference Test Success: Yamaha HS8 studio monitors demonstrated flat response (±3 dB 50Hz-16kHz) proving the room can achieve excellent performance with proper equipment
  • System Integration Impossible: 9 different brands with drastically different frequency responses cannot be EQ'd to match

4.2 Line Arrays vs Point Source Speakers - Research Results

✓ RESEARCH CONCLUSION: Line Arrays Superior for Reverberant Spaces

Academic testing in church with 2-second RT60 showed:

  • Line Arrays: Positive direct-to-reverberant ratio at all test positions
  • Line Arrays: Best C50 for critical speech bands (1kHz, 2kHz, 4kHz)
  • Point Source: Negative D/R ratio, muddy sound in rear areas
  • Conclusion: Line arrays "dramatically higher speech intelligibility" than point source
Characteristic Line Arrays Point Source/Columns
