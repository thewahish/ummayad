Historical Fire Damage & Restorations

The mosque has survived multiple fires throughout its history:

  • 1060, 1166, 1401, 1479, 1893 – Major fire incidents

5. Critical Issues Identified

5.1. Technical Issues

1. Fragmented Speaker System (9 Brands, 15-35 Years Old)

Impact Analysis

  • Inconsistent Frequency Response: Each brand/model has different tonal characteristics, making coherent coverage impossible
  • Varying Sensitivity: SPL differences of 20-25 dB between speaker types create "loud" and "quiet" zones
  • Impedance Mismatches: Mix of 4Ω, 8Ω, and 100V line speakers on same amplifier channels causes power distribution issues
  • Age Degradation: 20-30 year old speakers have deteriorated drivers, failed crossovers, and corroded terminals
  • No Design Cohesion: Ad-hoc additions over decades with no acoustic engineering oversight

2. Excessive Reverberation (RT60: 3.8-5.2 seconds)

The prayer hall's marble and stone construction creates an extremely reverberant environment:

  • Speech Intelligibility Destroyed: STI of 0.3-0.4 (Poor) means only 30-40% of words are understood clearly
  • Syllable Smearing: Reverberant tail from one word overlaps with the next, creating "muddy" sound
  • Cannot Fix Architecturally: Adding absorption materials would compromise UNESCO heritage status
  • SOLUTION REQUIRED: High-directivity speakers that minimize room excitation and maximize direct sound energy at listeners

6. Proposed System Design & Architecture

6.1. System Overview

The proposed sound system design addresses all identified technical and operational issues through a comprehensive, modern approach utilizing:

  • Unified Speaker System: Single brand/series for coherent acoustic performance
  • High-Directivity Design: Line arrays and steerable column arrays for reverberant space control
  • Digital Signal Processing (DSP): Precise time alignment, EQ, and feedback suppression
  • Dante Network Audio: Reliable digital distribution with redundancy
  • Zone-Based Architecture: Independent control of 6 major zones
  • Secure Configuration: Password protection and preset locking to prevent tampering

Design Philosophy

PRIMARY GOAL: Achieve Speech Transmission Index (STI) >0.6 in 90% of seating area despite 3.8-5.2s reverberation time by maximizing Direct-to-Reverberant ratio and minimizing late reflections.

10. Strategies to Improve Speech Intelligibility

Achieving acceptable speech intelligibility (STI >0.6) in a highly reverberant space (RT60 3.8-5.2s) requires a multi-faceted approach. The following strategies work together to maximize the Direct-to-Reverberant (D/R) ratio and minimize late reflections that destroy clarity.

10.1. Direct-to-Reverberant Ratio Optimization

The D/R Ratio Challenge

In reverberant spaces, speech intelligibility depends critically on the ratio of direct sound energy (from speaker to listener) versus reverberant sound energy (reflections from room surfaces). Research shows that:

  • D/R > +3 dB: Good intelligibility possible
  • D/R = 0 dB: Marginal intelligibility
  • D/R < -3 dB: Poor intelligibility (current situation in rear zones)

Strategies to Maximize D/R Ratio:

  1. High-Directivity Speakers: Line arrays and steerable columns focus energy toward listeners, not room surfaces
  2. Precise Aiming: Target listening plane (1.2m ear height for seated worshippers), avoid ceiling/dome
  3. Distributed Systems: Multiple zones with shorter throw distances maintain positive D/R throughout space
  4. High SPL Capability: Speakers must produce sufficient direct sound to overcome reverberant field
  5. Frequency-Dependent EQ: Boost critical speech bands (2-4 kHz) where intelligibility is most sensitive

10.2. Time Alignment & Delay

The Echo Problem

When sound from multiple speakers arrives at a listener with time differences >15ms, the brain perceives distinct echoes rather than a single coherent source. The current system has 28-45ms variance, causing severe intelligibility loss.

10.3. Frequency Response Management

The Speech Intelligibility Spectrum

Not all frequencies contribute equally to speech intelligibility. Research shows:

  • 125-250 Hz: Vowel fundamentals, adds warmth but limited intelligibility contribution
  • 500-1000 Hz: Vowel formants, important for speech naturalness
  • 2000-4000 Hz: Consonants, CRITICAL for intelligibility
  • 6000-8000 Hz: Sibilance (s, sh, f sounds), adds clarity
  • >10 kHz: Minimal speech content, often sacrificed to reduce noise

Proposed EQ Strategy by Zone

Zone-Specific Equalization

ALL ZONES:

  • High-Pass Filter: 80-100 Hz (-24 dB/octave) – Remove problematic low-frequency room modes and dome resonances
  • Presence Boost: +2 to +4 dB @ 2-4 kHz – Enhance critical speech intelligibility band
  • Sibilance Control: +1 to +2 dB @ 6-8 kHz – Add clarity without harshness

DISTANCE-DEPENDENT COMPENSATION:

  • Front Zones (0-45m): Flat response with minimal EQ beyond HPF
  • Mid Zones (45-90m): +2 dB per octave above 1 kHz (compensate for air absorption)
  • Rear Zones (90-136m): +3 dB per octave above 1 kHz (greater distance compensation)

ROOM MODE NOTCHING:

  • Identify specific resonant frequencies during commissioning (typically 80-200 Hz in large domed spaces)
  • Apply narrow parametric EQ cuts (Q=5-10) at identified modes
  • Reduces "boomy" low-frequency buildup that masks speech

11.3. Iterative Tuning

DSP Optimization Process

Based on post-installation measurements, the following iterative tuning process achieves optimal performance:

Tuning Workflow

  1. Phase 1: Level Setting
    • Set each zone's output level to achieve target SPL (75 dB)
    • Verify zone overlap transitions are smooth (no "hot spots" or "dead zones")
    • Set DSP limiters to prevent speaker overdrive
  2. Phase 2: Delay Alignment
    • Use impulse response measurement to verify acoustic arrival times
    • Adjust DSP delays for each zone within ±1ms precision
    • Walk test: Listen for echo-free transitions between zones
  3. Phase 3: Frequency Response EQ
    • Measure frequency response at multiple positions per zone
    • Apply parametric EQ to achieve target curve (presence boost, HPF, room mode notching)
    • Verify EQ changes don't create new problems in adjacent zones
  4. Phase 4: Feedback Suppression
    • Test all microphone positions (Mihrab, Minbar, Athan Room, wireless) at operating levels
    • Engage dbx AFS2 automatic feedback detection
    • Set fixed notch filters for identified feedback frequencies
    • Verify ≥6 dB gain margin before feedback at all positions
  5. Phase 5: Validation & Subjective Testing
    • Re-measure STI at all grid positions (confirm ≥0.6 target achieved)
    • Subjective testing with mosque staff, muadhins, and worshippers
    • Record "golden" settings as locked presets in DSP
    • Document all settings for future reference

Expected Tuning Duration

  • Initial Setup: 2-3 days (physical installation verification, basic level setting)
  • Measurement & Tuning: 5-7 days (grid measurements, DSP optimization, feedback testing)
  • Validation & Training: 2-3 days (final verification, staff training)
